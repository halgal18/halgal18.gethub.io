This is a meandering journey across the landscape of my life. The stories of my lifetime are filled with unexpected turns & twists and leaps & bounds. The audience of my tales has expanded over the years and I have been convinced to put some of my adventures down for perusal and potential mirth.

A Place to Ponder is a collection of adventure, mischief, and mayhem and how those experiences influenced me at each stage of my life. Not only is it a collection of both beautiful and ugly moments but a deep delve into the struggles against societal norms I have defied for more than half a century. The intent of the site is to leave a legacy of tales which share my frantic attempts to fit into a world of expected norms, while revealing the chaos within. I tie together the power struggles, the lessons learned, the subconscious shaping, and how my own narratives affect my everyday choices.

 

These pages will reveal:

    Quality: As my awareness expanded, I stumbled across a book titled Zen and the Art of Motorcycle Maintenance; a book that really isn’t about motorcycle maintenance and only a thin vein of Zen running through it. The primary point I got was Quality was the point of existence. I’ve never looked back.

    Dynamic Opposition: There seems to be a need for dynamic opposition in order to grow stronger in any organized framework. I seem to have elected myself as an armchair heretic, pushing against the Standard to test the temper of the ideas presented.

    Belief: Belief Systems (B.S.) generally curtails any further ability to learn about given (faith based) ideas. As a Skepticist I hope to be able to critically examine various perspectives without being caught up in the fury and passion of any particular BS.
